Hair Growth

So, you’re here because your hair won’t grow. Well…
Your hair is always growing. The problem is that your hair also breaks.

If your hair is breaking and growing at the same rate, the length will remain the same.
If your hair is breaking faster than it’s growing, it will get shorter, and if your hair barely breaks,
that’s when you see proper growth.

This post is not about miracle hair growth treatments and all that magical, mythical madness.
This is about ways to retain your hair’s length.



Why Won't My Hair Grow?!


6 possible reasons you’re not seeing growth



1. High Manipulation


High manipulation is when handling, styling and maintaining hair is done regularly.
This includes using heat and using a lot of tension… a massive no-no.


2. Brushing Dry Hair


There are only two instances where you can brush your hair thoughtlessly; when your hair is flat-ironed or blown out.
Other than that, you need to take your sweet, sweet time and pay attention to what you're doing.

Brushing your hair while it's wet and conditioned is the best method.
This ensures that your hair has slip, and that means those little knots are more likely to just slide right out.
When you brush through your curls while they're dry, you end up pulling out knots and creating unneeded tension.


3. Cotton Pillowcases


When you rest your pretty little head on cotton pillows, you’re summoning breakage.

What happens when you sleep is you move around, your hair moves around and the fluffy material on your pillowcase
sneakily snags your little hair strands.

Did somebody say “hell no”?

The material also soaks up the good stuff you have in your hair, leading to dry hair, which leads to… drumroll please.
Breakage!


4. Dryness


There are a lot of reasons your hair may be dry.

Washing your hair too often, using hot water on your hair and neglecting your beautiful mane all contribute to dryness.

Luckily, there are numerous ways to restore hydration.


5. Too Much Touching


This can cause frizziness. If your hair is already weak, touching it too often will definitely lead to breakage.


6. Not Enough Water


Your hair is a growing entity. It needs to be hydrated, just like you need hydration.
If you don't drink water, your body will make you wish you did. The same goes for your hair: if you neglect it, it will neglect you.



Things You Can Do


  • Low Manipulation Hairstyles



    The main aim of a low manipulation hairstyle is to keep your hands away from your hair.
    Wearing your hair loose often is a bad idea. This is because not only will you constantly (and most of the time,
    subliminally) be touching your hair, it will be exposed to wind and it will rub on your clothes…

    The horsemen of hair breakage.

    Putting your hair into a puff is a great way to avoid over manipulation. In this case, your ends are still exposed but
    your hair won’t get in your face and you have a much lower chance of ceaselessly touching your hair.


  • Wet Your Yair



    For me, it’s 100% mandatory to brush my hair only when it’s wet and has a comfortable amount of product on it.
    Even then, I take time to part my hair into sections and finger-detangle each section before going in with a brush.
    This helps you avoid breakage and unnecessary tension on your scalp.
    An easy way to do this is to brush your hair in the shower.
    Alternatively, you could fill a spray bottle with water and just spritz each section of hair as you go.
    One of my favourite things to do is mix conditioner in my spray bottle. It's quite the time-saver!


  • Invest in Satin



    Satin. What’s not to love?
    Sleeping on a satin/silk pillowcase gives your hair the freedom to move around seamlessly.
    Not only that, it also makes it easier for you to preserve hairstyles, retain moisture and prevent frizz.
    You can also use satin hair bonnets or wrap a satin scarf around your pillow.


  • Deep Condition



    One of the ways you can rehydrate your hair is to deep condition it.
    To intensify the effectiveness of your deep conditioner, while it’s in your hair, put a plastic shower cap
    on your head, then wrap it all up in a towel.
    Then you can leave your hair like that so it can soak up all the yummy goodness of your deep conditioner.
    If you don’t have a plastic shower cap, you can use a plastic bag.
    The reason we use a plastic cover is that it traps the heat that radiates from your head, which helps your hair
    follicles absorb the product even better.


  • Hands Off



    Try styling your hair in ways that require a minimal amount of manipulation.
    Keep your hair out of your face so you don't have to constantly touch your hair to move it out of your face.
    Tie your hair in a low bun. This will keep the ends of your hair protected, and reduce breakage.


  • Stay Hydrated



    I've said it before, and I'll say it again... Your hair needs water, just like you.
    If your hair is dehydrated, it will lose the strength it needs to stay in tact.
    So be sure to stay hydrated to make sure your hair does too.
