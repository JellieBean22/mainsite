Conquering Thin Hair

You want thicker hair. I get it… Thin hair just makes you feel like your hair’s unhealthy.
So, we’ll start this off by going through why your hair is thin



Why is My Hair So Thin?!


Like with many other things in life, there are multiple possible causes


Lets take a look at 6 possible causes


1. STRESS!


I can’t tell you how many times I’ve looked for advice on how to deal with thin hair and gotten this; “stop stressing”.

Is it just me, or is that the most useless piece of advice?

So, I won’t tell you to stop stressing, but I will try and help you manage it


2. Harsh Products


If you’re using an intense shampoo and/or using it regularly, this will strip your hair of its natural oils.

I’ve said it before, and I’ll say it again - Baby. Shampoo.

It’s gentle on your scalp, does the job and it’s inexpensive. You don’t need to use it all the time, maybe use it for every second wash day.


3. Neglect


You know how people say that a child’s behaviour is a reflection of their parents?

Well, your hair’s appearance is generally a reflection of the love you give it.

With that being said – avoid over-styling your hair because constant pulling and over-brushing is not good for her.


4. Lifestyle


Do you exercise? How much water do you drink every day? Are you getting enough protein?

I know this can be frustrating to hear if 1) your answer is yes, and your hair is still thin or 2) you’ve tried it before, and it didn’t work.

This might be due to:

  • genetics
  • health complications
  • inconsistency

5. Pregnancy


Don't freak out! Thin hair is not a sign of pregnancy.

Pregnancy-related hair loss commonly occurs after your baby’s birth instead of during pregnancy.

The good news is this happens because of hormonal changes, so it generally resolves on its own.


6. Age


“But I can’t fix that, Jay!” Yes, true. You can’t make yourself stop ageing.

But there are things you can do to slow down the lovely curve balls age throws at you.



Now that that's done, let's see what we can do to save your hair!



Things You Can Do

  • Managing Stress



    • Learn how to manage your time.
      Not having enough time to do anything can cause insurmountable stress.
      Time management is super effective. It makes you feel so much lighter and like you can do so much more.

    • Get social!
      Go enjoy the company of your friends and family. You need it and so do they.
      Being around people you love can sometimes be exactly what you need to make you feel awesome.

    • Be nice to yourself.
      Don’t talk bad to yourself. Don't talk bad about yourself.
      Dedicate some of your time each week to selfcare.
      Have a bubble bath
      Go get a massage
      Meditate

There are countless ways to manage your stress. If you’d like to learn more, let me know!


  • The Right Products



    • Products with olive oil, castor oil and/or eggs are super good for your locs
    • Jamaican Black Castor oil is amazing. Many people shy away from it because of its thick consistency, but never fear –
      essential oils are here! If you don’t like the thickness, you can thin it out a little with essential oils.
      Keep in mind, a little bit goes a long way.
    • Lavender and Rosemary oil are your besties. Just a drop or two will dilute thick oils nicely.

  • Show Some Love



    • Pay attention to the ends of your hair. The ends are the driest and the oldest so they need the most love.
      When you apply oil, pay more, if not, all your attention to your ends.
    • Don’t be shy with conditioner. The lengths and ends of your hair need all the conditioner you can give them.
      Your roots are lucky enough to benefit from natural oils right at the source, so you don't really need to condition your roots.

  • Try Something New



    • If your hair is thin because of genetics, there isn’t really anything you can do to thicken it.
      You can, however, make the illusion that your hair is thick. How? Extensions!
      Experiment with hair pieces so you can make your hair look as thick as you’d like
    • Eat, eat, eat
      There are a lot of foods that can help with thinning hair. Allow me to list a few:

      • Strawberries, citrus fruits and guavas – these are great sources of vitamin C.
        Having low levels of vitamin C has been linked to poor health outcomes.
      • White beans – these hold a great deal of iron.
        Many doctors actually test their patients for iron deficiencies when they complain about thinning hair
      • Brown rice, broccoli and avocados – lovely vitamin B.
        This helps strengthen your hair, and strong hair has a lower risk of breaking (duh).

The list really does go on. So, if you’d like a more detailed post on this, let me know and I will gladly put one together.


  • Make a Change



    • For my older ladies, gents and everything in between - antioxidants may help you and your hair handle hormonal changes.
      Antioxidants help shield your body from the sun and seal moisture into your hair, which retains the natural oils in your scalp.
    • If you have grey hair, be consistent with your deep conditioning. Unfortunately, grey hair is more susceptible to breakage.
    • Speak to your doctor. You could possibly be using medication that’s making your hair break.


Important:

Thin hair refers to density.
Fine hair is the thickness of your hair’s shaft.
There is a difference between thin and fine hair.
It’s basically about the amount of hair you have.
