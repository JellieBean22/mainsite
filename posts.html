Tackling Dry Hair

Having dry hair is never fun & it just ain’t cute. Luckily, it can be managed.
Here are some tips and tricks to help you ditch dry hair without spending heaps of cash.



Why is My Hair So Dry?!


Let's look at 6 possible causes


1. Too Much Heat


Super obvious but overlooked quite often.

Imagine sitting out in the harsh sun a couple times a month without any sunscreen or after-sun care (like aloe vera).
You would have really dry & sensitive skin.

The same goes for your hair.
If you’re blow drying and flat ironing your hair regularly, even with heat protectant, your hair will start to suffer.


2. Chemicals



The mere thought of using chemicals on your precious locs is terrifying and that’s because when we think “chemicals”,
we think of super harsh, stinky, toxic products. But many modern chemical hair products are a little deceiving.


3. Chlorine

That's right! Those late night dips do more than just cool you down.

But don’t get me wrong, swimming is awesome. The only reason it will dry out your hair is if you don’t wash the chlorine
out after a dip in the pool.


4. Shampoo


I know, I know… I just told you to wash your hair.

But washing your hair is great! What isn’t great is washing your hair too often. How often is too often? Well, it depends.
For me, three times a week is too excessive. But some people wash their hair every second day with no problem.
Here is where you need to listen to your hair. Only wash it when you need to, and use gentle poos.

If I need to wash my hair more than once in a week, then I use baby shampoo; it does the job without stripping my hair of
all its natural oily goodness.

Give it a try!


5. The Weather


"But Jay, I can’t avoid the weather!” – is that what you’re thinking? I bet it is.

Don't worry, you don’t need to avoid crazy hot and windy weather, just protect your hair from it.
For example, I carry a scarf with me that I can use as a headwrap if the wind decides to be disrespectful.

For those of you with longer hair, you can throw your hair into a bun to at least protect the tips of your hair.


6. Using the Wrong Products


One of the best pieces of hair advice I’ve been offered is: Use a shampoo for the hair you have and conditioner for the hair you want.
I feel like that tip is good enough on its own, but if you’d like an elaboration, feel free to let me know!



Now that the possible causes are out of the way, let's jump into how to fix the problem



What Can I Do


  • Take a Break from Heat



    Plain and simple. Like I said before; listen to your hair. If she says heat ain’t it, then that’s that.
    If you use heat on your hair 4 times a month, try cut back to twice and see how that small change makes a big difference.
    I know a lot of naturals who only use heat once a year, and their hair is thriving.
    I understand that using heat once a year sounds like too much of a change for many of you, so just try cutting back
    gradually and your hair will certainly thank you.


  • Embrace Your Natural Hair Colour



    Dye is really tempting, and I have often fallen victim to its seduction, but there is one thing I notice a month or 2 after
    dyeing my mane: I miss my natural colour!
    There is one thing you can do if you simply can’t stay away from dyes; buy some wigs. A good wig is not cheap, but
    it’s definitely worth it. The same goes for other chemical treatments like relaxers, straighteners, Brazilians, etc.
    Just ask yourself if your hair truly needs to go through all that damage.


  • If You are a Regular Swimmer...



    ...it’s a bad idea to wash your hair every time you swim.
    This is not to say you should just leave that nasty chlorine in your hair, instead you should buy baby shampoo or a gentle
    cleansing shampoo to get the chlorine out without drying your hair.


  • Use Shampoo Sparingly



    A lot of people believe that the more hair you have, the more shampoo you need.
    This is undeniable nonsense!
    You only need enough shampoo to wash your scalp (so if your head is huge then you can disregard my above statement)
    because this is where all your build-up is.
    When you rinse out your shampoo, enough of it will touch on your length and tips without drying it out.


  • Wrap it Up



    There are countless tutorials on how to do headwraps and turbans, and if you say you don’t look good with your hair wrapped,
    you’re lying. When it’s hot and/or windy out, show your hair some love with hair food/deep conditioner, throw on a turban and
    go about your day.
    Not only will this protect your hair from the elements, but it will also lock moisture into your hair and in many cases, soften it.
    What’s not to love!


  • Do Not be shy with your Conditioner.



    That bottle of liquid gold is your hair’s best friend. Around once a week, I like to saturate my hair in my favourite deep conditioner
    and throw on a shower cap under my microfiber towel. I let that sit for an hour and my hair loves it.
    There are also different hair masks you can try out, but this is usually a trial-and-error process. Some naturals swear against coconut
    oil masks because their hair just doesn’t like coconut oil. On the other hand, a lot of naturals are crazy for coconut oil.
    So, it all boils down to what your hair likes.


Some Tips


  1. Before you spend your life’s savings on shampoos, conditioners and leave in conditioners, look at the ingredients.
    You always want water/aqua to be the very first ingredient. Water is an amazing moisturizer!

  2. Invest in a satin/silk pillowcase. Your regular pillowcase absorbs your hair’s moisture, which is not what we want.

  3. Take your vitamins.

  4. Stay hydrated.
